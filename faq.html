Admissions

Eligibility varies by course. As a general guide:

  • CPL / ATPL Ground School: 10+2 (Class 12) with Physics and Mathematics. Minimum age 17 years.
  • EASA Part 66 / DGCA AME: 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics, or a relevant engineering degree (B.E./B.Tech). Minimum age 18 years.
  • Cabin Crew / Ground Staff / Ticketing: 10+2 minimum. Some airlines prefer graduates.
  • Flight Dispatcher / Radio Telephony: 10+2 or equivalent.

For Ex-IAF / defence personnel, special eligibility pathways are available. Please contact our admissions team for personalized guidance.

Age requirements are course-specific:

  • CPL Ground School: Minimum 17 years at the time of enrollment.
  • EASA Part 66: Minimum 18 years.
  • Cabin Crew Training: Typically 18–27 years (as per airline hiring standards), though training enrollment is open to eligible candidates.
  • Ground Staff / Ticketing / Cargo: 18+ years preferred.
  • Ex-IAF / Defence veterans: No upper age restriction for EASA or DGCA AME conversion programs.

Our admissions process is simple and student-friendly:

  • Step 1: Submit an enquiry via our contact form, call us or WhatsApp us.
  • Step 2: Attend a free counselling session with our advisors to identify the right course for your goals.
  • Step 3: Submit your documents and complete the admission formalities.
  • Step 4: Receive your batch confirmation and begin training on your scheduled start date.

We admit students on a rolling basis. Contact us to know the next available batch date for your course of interest.

Typically required documents include:

  • 10th and 12th mark sheets / certificates
  • Degree certificate (if applicable)

  • Passport-size photographs (4 copies)

Specific document requirements may vary by course. Our admissions team will provide the complete checklist upon enquiry.

EASA Part 66

EASA Part 66 is the European Aviation Safety Agency standard for certifying Aircraft Maintenance Technicians (AMTs) and Aircraft Maintenance Engineers (AMEs). It is the most widely recognized international aircraft maintenance license, valid across European Union countries and recognized globally by many aviation authorities.

Holding an EASA Part 66 license significantly expands your career opportunities — enabling you to work with European airlines, international MROs, and airlines worldwide that operate EASA-registered aircraft.

B1 (Mechanical/Structural): Covers airframe and powerplant maintenance. Sub-categories include B1.1 (Aeroplane Turbine — most common) and B1.3 (Helicopter Turbine). B1 engineers certify mechanical maintenance tasks.

B2 (Avionics): Covers aircraft avionics and electrical systems. B2 engineers are qualified to certify avionics and electrical maintenance tasks including navigation, communication and flight management systems.

At Vajra Aviation, we offer training for B1.1, B1.3 and B2 in partnership with Aviotrace Swiss. Many students pursue both B1 and B2 to enhance employability.

Placements

Yes. Placement support is a core commitment at Vajra Aviation — we don't just train, we actively work to place our graduates. Our placement assistance includes:

  • Resume building and interview preparation workshops
  • Direct referrals to airline and MRO HR departments
  • Campus interviews and recruitment drives

  • IndiGo — India's largest airline (CPL, ATPL, AME, Cabin Crew, Ground Staff)
