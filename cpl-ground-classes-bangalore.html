Common Questions About CPL Ground Classes

Where can I find CPL ground classes near me in Bangalore?
Vajra Aviation is located at NO.95, Third Floor, G R Arcade, Sector-C, Amrutha Nagar, Amruthalli, Bangalore 560092 — easily accessible from Yelahanka, Hebbal, Nagawara, and North Bangalore. Call 6364919234 to check the current batch schedule and visit the centre.
What makes Vajra Aviation the best pilot training institute in Bangalore?
Vajra Aviation stands out because of its Ex-IAF faculty (retired Sqn Ldrs with 10–30 years experience), small batch sizes (max 15 students), DGCA exam-pattern focus, ISO certification, and strong placement network. Unlike many institutes that hire generic teachers, every class at Vajra is taken by someone who has actually served in the Indian Air Force.
How long are the CPL ground classes at Vajra Aviation?
The regular CPL ground school batch runs for 6 months covering all 7 DGCA subjects. Intensive weekend batches are also available for working professionals or students who are simultaneously completing their flying training at a flying school. Mock exams and revision sessions are included.
Can I join CPL ground classes while doing my flying training?
Yes, absolutely. In fact, it is recommended to complete your CPL ground school (theory) parallel to your flying training. DGCA requires both theory and practical (flying hours) for the CPL. Vajra Aviation's batch timings are flexible to accommodate students doing simultaneous flying training.
What is the fee for CPL ground classes in Bangalore?
CPL ground class fees at Vajra Aviation are competitively priced with EMI options available. Contact us at 6364919234 or visit the centre for the current fee structure and batch dates. We also offer scholarship options for meritorious students.
Is ground school enough to get a CPL?
Ground school covers the theory component (7 written exams) required for DGCA CPL. You also need to complete flying training at a DGCA-approved flying school (minimum 200 hours) and pass a skill test. Vajra Aviation handles the ground school — the academic foundation that determines your success in all DGCA written exams.

Most other institutes use general teachers with aviation book knowledge.
