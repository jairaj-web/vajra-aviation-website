What does a flight dispatcher do?
A flight dispatcher (also called flight operations officer or FOO) is responsible for pre-flight planning, fuel calculations, weather analysis, filing flight plans, and monitoring flights in progress. In India, DGCA-licensed dispatchers are required at all scheduled airlines. The dispatcher shares responsibility for flight safety with the Pilot-in-Command — a legally important and operationally critical role.
How long is flight dispatcher training at Vajra Aviation?
The flight dispatcher course at Vajra Aviation runs for 6 months, covering all 7 DGCA subjects with mock exams and practical flight planning exercises. Classes run Monday to Saturday. Batch dates — call 6364919234 for the next available intake.
What is the eligibility for the DGCA Flight Dispatcher licence?
Minimum age 21, 10+2 pass, no criminal record. DGCA requires a written examination covering all dispatcher subjects plus practical training at an approved organisation. No Class 1 medical is required — making this an accessible aviation career for those without the flying medical fitness. Graduates are preferred by airlines but are not mandatory for the licence itself.
What salary does a flight dispatcher earn in India?
Entry-level flight dispatchers in India earn ₹3–5 LPA at regional airlines. Senior dispatchers at major airlines like IndiGo and Air India earn ₹8–15 LPA. Demand is high due to India's rapid airline expansion — IndiGo alone is adding 100+ aircraft. Visit our placements page to see where our graduates work.
Is flight dispatcher a good career in India?
Excellent career — India's aviation sector is the fastest growing in the world. Every airline needs licensed dispatchers for every flight operated. Unlike the pilot career, there is no mandatory medical restriction, no simulator hours requirement, and the career is less physically demanding. Strong job security and steady salary growth with experience.
Can a pilot also get a flight dispatcher licence?
Yes — many CPL holders get the dispatcher licence as a backup qualification. The dispatcher role uses many of the same skills as the pilot (flight planning, weather analysis, ATC procedures) and is an excellent fallback if flying is temporarily unavailable. Vajra Aviation offers both CPL Ground School and Flight Dispatcher training. Pilots with a dispatcher licence are significantly more employable in airline operations roles.
