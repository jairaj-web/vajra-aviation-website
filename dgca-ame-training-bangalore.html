What is DGCA AME training and why do I need it?
DGCA AME (Aircraft Maintenance Engineer) training prepares you for the Indian DGCA licence required to inspect and certify aircraft as airworthy. Without this licence you cannot legally sign off maintenance on Indian-registered aircraft. The training covers airframe, engines, avionics, electrical systems, air regulations, hydraulics, and meteorology over 2 years with DGCA exam preparation throughout.
How long is the DGCA AME training at Vajra Aviation?
The DGCA AME programme at Vajra Aviation runs for 2 years, covering all 8 modules with dedicated DGCA exam preparation, previous year paper practice, and full-length mock tests for each module. Batches start every month — contact us for the next available batch date.
What jobs can I get after completing DGCA AME training?
After DGCA AME training and licence, career options include: aircraft engineer at airlines (IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet, Vistara), MRO companies (Air Works, GMR Aviation, Air India Engineering Services), defence aviation contractors, DGCA inspection roles, and helicopter operators. Starting salaries range from ₹4–8 LPA with strong growth prospects.
What is the difference between DGCA AME and EASA Part 66?
DGCA AME is the Indian licence valid for VT-registered (Indian) aircraft, required to work in Indian airlines and MROs. EASA Part 66 is the European licence with international recognition — valid for work on European-registered aircraft and accepted by many global airlines and MROs. Vajra Aviation offers both programmes. Read our detailed DGCA AME vs EASA Part 66 comparison.
