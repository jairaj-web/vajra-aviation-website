Course Overview

Airport ground staff are the backbone of airline operations. From the moment a passenger arrives at the airport to the moment the aircraft pushes back from the gate, ground staff ensure that everything runs safely, on time, and professionally. With India's aviation sector growing at 8–10% annually, the demand for trained, professional ground staff has never been higher.

Vajra Aviation's Airport Ground Staff Training is a comprehensive program that covers all operational areas of airport ground handling. Our curriculum is designed to meet the standards demanded by India's top airlines and ground handling companies. Students graduate with practical skills, professional grooming, and the Vajra Aviation placement network behind them.

Course Curriculum

A practical, operations-focused curriculum covering every ground handling function:

Module 1: Introduction to Airport Operations
  • Airport layout — terminals, gates, aprons, runways
  • Types of airlines and ground handling companies
  • Airport regulatory authority — AAI, BCAS, customs
  • Airport security overview and access procedures
  • Role of ground staff in the on-time performance (OTP) chain
Module 2: Passenger Handling & Customer Service
  • Passenger types — normal, unaccompanied minors, VIPs, passengers with reduced mobility
  • Customer service standards and airline service etiquette
  • Passenger complaint handling and escalation procedures
  • Communication skills and professional language
  • Cultural sensitivity and passenger diversity
Module 3: Check-in & Boarding Procedures
  • Departure control systems (DCS) — check-in and boarding workflow
  • Seat assignment, boarding pass issuance
  • Boarding gate procedures and final checks
  • Weight and balance considerations at check-in
  • No-show and overbooking management procedures
Module 4: Ramp Operations
  • Aircraft marshalling and parking procedures
  • Ground support equipment (GSE) overview
  • Aircraft turnaround procedures — fuelling, catering, cleaning
  • Pushback and towing procedures
  • Ramp safety, FOD prevention, and hazard awareness
Module 5: Baggage Handling
  • Baggage acceptance standards — size, weight, restricted items
  • Baggage make-up and loading procedures
  • Baggage reconciliation system (BRS)
  • Mishandled baggage — tracing, claims, delivery
  • Dangerous goods in baggage — identification and handling
Module 6: Dangerous Goods & Safety
  • IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations (DGR) awareness
  • Categories of dangerous goods and their handling
  • Emergency procedures at the airport
  • Fire safety and evacuation procedures
  • First aid awareness for airport personnel
Module 7: Personality Development & Grooming
  • Professional grooming standards for airline ground staff
  • Body language and communication in airport environment
  • Uniform and personal hygiene standards
  • Mock airline interview preparation
  • Resume building and career planning for ground staff

Eligibility Criteria

  • 10+2 (HSC) in any stream from a recognized board
  • Age: 18 to 28 years at time of enrollment
  • Minimum height: 157 cm (female), 170 cm (male) — preferred for most airlines
  • Good communication skills in English and Hindi
  • Pleasant personality and customer-service orientation
  • Graduates and diploma holders are also eligible

Career Outcomes

Our Ground Staff graduates are employed in high-demand roles across India's fastest-growing airports:

  • Airport Check-in Agent at domestic and international airlines
  • Ramp Agent / Ground Handling Agent at airlines and ground handling companies
  • Passenger Services Agent (PSA) at airports
  • Baggage Handler and Tracing Agent
  • Airport Lounge and VIP Services Personnel
Salary Potential

Entry-level ground staff earn ₹15,000–25,000/month with benefits. Senior ground handling professionals and supervisors earn ₹30,000–50,000/month.
