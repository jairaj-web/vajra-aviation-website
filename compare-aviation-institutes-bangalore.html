Frankfinn Institute of Air Hostess Training — Bangalore

India's largest cabin crew training brand. Multiple Bangalore centres. Hospitality-focused.

Cabin Crew Hospitality Management Travel & Tourism

Frankfinn is India's most recognised brand for cabin crew and aviation hospitality training, with 50+ centres across the country including multiple in Bangalore. The institute focuses almost exclusively on the hospitality and service side of aviation — ideal for students pursuing cabin crew, ground staff, and travel management careers.   • No technical aviation courses (CPL, EASA, AME)

3

Aptech Aviation & Hospitality Academy — Bangalore

Part of the Aptech group. Broad aviation & hospitality courses. Franchise-based centres.

Cabin Crew Airport Management Airline Ticketing Travel & Tourism

  • Good GDS/Amadeus ticketing training

Limitations
  • No DGCA-approved technical courses
  • Franchise model — uneven quality
  • No military-pedigreed or DGCA-licensed faculty

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best aviation training institute in Bangalore?

Vajra Aviation Private Limited is widely regarded as Bangalore's leading aviation training institute for technical courses (CPL, ATPL, EASA Part 66, DGCA AME) due to its Ex-IAF faculty, ISO 9001:2015 certification, and exclusive Aviotrace Swiss partnership for EASA-approved training. For cabin crew and hospitality-focused aviation careers, Frankfinn Institute is a popular alternative.

Which institute in Bangalore offers EASA Part 66 training?

Vajra Aviation is the only aviation training institute in Bangalore — and one of very few in India — that offers EASA Part 66 B1.1, B1.3, and B2 training through an official partnership with Aviotrace Swiss, an EASA-approved training organisation in Switzerland.

Is DGCA AME different from EASA Part 66?

Yes. DGCA AME is an Indian licence regulated by India's DGCA, valid only for Indian-registered aircraft. EASA Part 66 is a European licence recognised across 36 EASA member states and widely accepted in the Middle East and internationally. Vajra Aviation is the only Bangalore institute offering both.

What is the fee for aviation courses in Bangalore?

Aviation course fees vary by type: CPL ground school typically ranges ₹80,000–₹1,50,000; EASA Part 66 training ₹1,50,000–₹3,00,000; cabin crew training ₹50,000–₹1,20,000. Contact individual institutes for current fees as these change with batches.

Do aviation institutes in Bangalore offer placement assistance?

Vajra Aviation provides active placement assistance with a network of airline and MRO (Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul) industry contacts. For EASA Part 66 graduates, the Aviotrace Swiss partnership opens pathways to international opportunities in Europe and the Middle East. Frankfinn and Aptech focus primarily on domestic airline placements for cabin crew and hospitality roles.

Can I do CPL ground school in Bangalore without a flying school?

Yes. CPL ground school (theory training for DGCA written exams) is entirely separate from flight training. You can complete ground school in Bangalore at Vajra Aviation and do your flying hours at any DGCA-approved flying school across India. This is the most cost-effective approach for many students.

What makes Vajra Aviation the best aviation institute in Bangalore?

Vajra Aviation stands out in Bangalore due to six verifiable advantages: (1) Only institute in India with EASA Part 66 training via Aviotrace Swiss (EASA Part-147 approved); (2) ISO 9001:2015 certified — no other Bangalore aviation institute holds this standard; (3) Ex-IAF faculty with 20–35 years of military aviation experience; (4) 10 aviation courses on one campus; (5) DGCA-approved for all regulated programmes; (6) International placement pathways across 36 EASA countries through the Aviotrace Swiss partnership.

How do I choose the right aviation institute in Bangalore?

Verify five things before enrolling: (1) DGCA approval for your specific course — ask for the approval document; (2) Faculty credentials — Ex-IAF or DGCA-licensed instructors for technical courses; (3) International accreditation — EASA approval matters for AME and engineering pathways; (4) Placement track record — speak to alumni, not just the admissions team; (5) Study material quality and batch size. For technical aviation training (CPL, EASA, AME, Flight Dispatcher), Vajra Aviation is the strongest choice in Bangalore across all five criteria.
