Common Questions About EASA Part 66 Training

Where can I find EASA Part 66 training near me in Bangalore?

What is the difference between EASA Part 66 B1.1 and B2?
EASA Part 66 B1.1 is the mechanical category — covering airframe, engines, and mechanical systems. Maintenance engineers with B1.1 sign off mechanical and structural work. B2 is the avionics category — covering electrical, electronic, digital, and instrument systems. B2 engineers sign off avionics and electrical work. Both licences are issued by EASA and valid across 30+ countries.
Is EASA Part 66 valid in India?
EASA Part 66 is primarily a European licence, but it is widely recognised by airlines and MROs in the Middle East, Europe, UK, and South-East Asia. In India, DGCA recognises it for certain approvals. Many Indian engineers hold both DGCA AME and EASA Part 66 licences to maximise their career options globally.
What is the salary for EASA Part 66 engineers?
EASA Part 66 engineers earn significantly more than DGCA AME holders due to international recognition. Entry-level B1.1/B2 engineers in India earn ₹4–8 LPA. In the Middle East (UAE, Oman, Qatar), tax-free packages range from AED 6,000–15,000/month (₹14–35 LPA equivalent). European MRO positions offer €35,000–65,000/year for certified engineers.
How long does EASA Part 66 B1.1 training take?
EASA Part 66 B1.1 training at Vajra Aviation covers 13 core modules over 18–24 months. This includes classroom instruction, mock exams, and practical orientation. The actual EASA licence requires 3 years of post-training practical experience on type-certified aircraft under approved Part 145 supervision.
What is Aviotrace Swiss and why does it matter?
Aviotrace Swiss is a EASA-approved Maintenance Training Organisation (MTO) based in Switzerland, approved under EASA Part 147. Their approval means that training conducted under their authorisation meets the highest European aviation standards. Vajra Aviation is the only institute in India authorised by Aviotrace Swiss — giving students internationally credible EASA Part 66 preparation.
Can I do EASA Part 66 and DGCA AME together?
Yes — many of the core subjects overlap between EASA Part 66 and DGCA AME. Vajra Aviation's curriculum is structured so students can pursue both simultaneously or sequentially. This dual qualification significantly increases employability — both in India (DGCA-regulated airlines) and internationally (EASA MROs).
Which airlines and MROs hire EASA Part 66 engineers?
EASA Part 66 B1.1/B2 engineers are hired by Lufthansa Technik, Air France Industries KLM, Etihad Airways MRO, Emirates Engineering, SR Technics, and Singapore Airlines Engineering Company. Indian carriers like Air India and IndiGo also recognise EASA qualifications for international fleet maintenance roles.
