How to Become Cabin Crew in India — Step by Step

A career as cabin crew is one of the most in-demand aviation careers in India. IndiGo, Air India, Akasa Air, and SpiceJet hire thousands of cabin crew every year. Here is how to get there:

1
Complete 10+2 from Any Stream
Unlike pilot training, cabin crew does not require Physics or Maths. Any 10+2 stream is accepted — Science, Commerce, or Arts. A degree (graduation) is preferred by some airlines but not mandatory for most domestic carriers.
2
Meet Physical Requirements
Airlines have specific requirements: minimum height 157 cm (female) or 170 cm (male), proportionate weight, good vision (correctable), no visible tattoos, and an overall pleasant appearance. Meeting these from day one saves time in the process.
3
Enroll in a Certified Cabin Crew Training Program
Join a certified cabin crew training institute in Bangalore like Vajra Aviation. Complete all 6 modules — Aviation Fundamentals, Safety & Emergency, Grooming, Customer Service, First Aid, and Airline Interview Preparation. Duration: 3–6 months.
4
Apply for Airline Cabin Crew Openings
Apply directly via airline career portals — IndiGo (goindigo.in), Air India (airindia.com), Akasa Air (akasaair.com). Also attend walk-in recruitment drives in Bangalore, Chennai, Mumbai, and Delhi. Our placement team notifies you of every drive.
5
Clear the Airline Selection Process
Airline selection involves: Document Screening → Written Test → Group Discussion → Personal Interview → Grooming Round → Medical Check. Vajra Aviation's training covers every stage. Our 95%+ selection rate proves the preparation works.
6
Complete Airline Initial Training (AIT)
Once selected, the airline conducts its own Initial Training (AIT) — typically 4–8 weeks of safety, emergency, and service training specific to their aircraft. Students who complete certified cabin crew training at Vajra Aviation consistently perform better in AIT and pass on the first attempt.
7
Start Your Career as Cabin Crew
You are now a qualified cabin crew member. Fresher salary: ₹25,000–₹35,000/month plus flying allowances. With experience (2–3 years), salaries rise to ₹50,000–₹70,000+/month. International postings push packages even higher.

What Airlines Actually Test in Cabin Crew Selection

Airlines reject candidates who only focus on looks and grooming. Here is what the actual selection process tests at each stage — and how Vajra Aviation prepares you for every round:

1
Document Screening

Airlines verify your 10+2 certificate, age proof, passport, and any aviation training certificates. A Vajra Aviation Cabin Crew Training Certificate significantly improves your shortlisting chances over untrained applicants.

2
Written Test — Safety & General Knowledge

Tests aviation safety knowledge, emergency procedures, and situational judgment. Candidates who have completed structured safety training at certified institutes consistently outperform untrained applicants here.

3
Group Discussion (GD)

Evaluates communication clarity, teamwork, leadership presence, and confidence under group pressure. Vajra Aviation conducts dedicated GD practice sessions with feedback — this stage eliminates 60–70% of candidates at major airlines.

4
Personal Interview (PI)

HR and operations managers test your motivation, ability to handle difficult passengers, conflict resolution, and situational responses. Our Module 6 covers 50+ standard interview questions with model answers, recorded mock interviews, and real-time feedback sessions.

5
Grooming & Physical Assessment

Airlines conduct a structured grooming check — hair, makeup, uniform presentation, posture, walk, and reach test (overhead locker test). Vajra Aviation trains students to meet airline-grade grooming standards from week one of training.

6
Medical Examination

Final medical check confirms vision, hearing, BMI, and absence of conditions that could affect passenger safety. This is the final hurdle — and it is pass/fail. Our pre-course guidance helps students prepare medically before applying.

Cabin Crew Training FAQs

What is cabin crew training?

Cabin crew training is a professional course that prepares candidates to work as flight attendants on commercial airlines. It covers aviation safety, emergency procedures, grooming, customer service, first aid, and airline interview preparation. Completing certified cabin crew training is the fastest way to get hired by Indian airlines.

What is the eligibility for cabin crew training in Bangalore?

To join cabin crew training at Vajra Aviation you must have passed 10+2 (any stream), be between 18–27 years of age, have a minimum height of 157 cm (female) or 170 cm (male), be fluent in English and Hindi, and hold a valid passport. Indian nationality is preferred for domestic airlines.

How long does cabin crew training take?

Cabin crew training at Vajra Aviation takes 3–6 months depending on the batch type. Our regular batch covers all 6 modules across 3 months of full-time training. Extended batches with additional interview practice and airline mock sessions run up to 6 months.

What airlines can I join after completing cabin crew training?

Vajra Aviation graduates have been placed with IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet, Akasa Air, Vistara, and Air Arabia. Our placement team actively connects students with airline recruitment drives throughout India. Our 95%+ placement rate is the result of our strong industry network and interview preparation curriculum.

Is cabin crew training the same as air hostess training?

Yes. “Cabin crew training” and “air hostess training” refer to the same professional programme. “Cabin crew” is the official DGCA and IATA term that includes both male (flight steward) and female (flight attendant/air hostess) roles. The training curriculum is identical.

What is the salary of cabin crew in India?

Entry-level cabin crew at Indian airlines earn between ₹25,000–₹60,000 per month plus flying allowances, travel benefits, and accommodation support. Senior cabin crew and purser roles at airlines like Air India earn ₹70,000–₹1,20,000/month. International airline cabin crew earn significantly higher packages.

Does Vajra Aviation provide placement assistance after training?

Yes. Vajra Aviation provides direct placement assistance including interview preparation, grooming assessments, mock airline interviews, and access to our airline network. We notify students about recruitment drives at IndiGo, Air India, Akasa Air, SpiceJet, and other carriers. Our 95%+ placement rate reflects genuine outcomes.

Do I need prior experience for cabin crew training?

No prior aviation experience is needed. Vajra Aviation's cabin crew training is designed for freshers — candidates who have just completed 10+2 or graduation. Our course takes you from zero aviation knowledge to airline-ready in 3–6 months.
