Course Curriculum

Six comprehensive modules designed to make you airline-ready from day one:

Module 1: Aviation Fundamentals
  • History and structure of commercial aviation
  • Aircraft types and cabin configurations
  • Role and responsibilities of cabin crew
  • Airline industry overview — DGCA, IATA, ICAO
  • Aviation terminology and communication basics
Module 2: Safety & Emergency Procedures
  • Aircraft evacuation procedures and commands
  • Emergency equipment — life jackets, rafts, escape slides
  • Fire fighting in the cabin — types and procedures
  • Decompression and oxygen equipment usage
  • Turbulence and water ditching procedures
  • Handling disruptive passengers and hijack protocols
Module 3: Grooming & Personality Development
  • Airline grooming standards — hair, makeup, uniform
  • Personal hygiene and appearance guidelines
  • Posture, deportment, and professional body language
  • Communication skills — verbal and non-verbal
  • Confidence building and public speaking
  • Cultural sensitivity and etiquette
Module 4: Customer Service Excellence
  • Passenger handling — boarding, in-flight, deplaning
  • Meal and beverage service techniques
  • Handling special passengers — infants, elderly, PRM
  • Conflict resolution and complaint management
  • Premium class service standards — business and first class
  • Cultural awareness for international flights
Module 5: First Aid & Medical Emergencies
  • Basic life support — CPR and AED usage
  • In-flight medical emergencies — heart attack, stroke, epilepsy
  • Childbirth assistance procedures
  • First aid kit contents and usage
  • Oxygen administration and medical reporting
Module 6: Airline Interview Preparation
  • Airline selection process — written, GD, PI, and medical
  • Resume writing and application guidelines
  • Common interview questions and ideal responses
  • Mock interviews with video feedback
  • Group discussion techniques and leadership presence
  • Dress code, walk, and presentation for airline interviews

Eligibility Criteria

  • 10+2 (HSC) from a recognised board in any stream
  • Age: 18 to 27 years at time of enrollment
  • Minimum height: 157 cm (female) / 170 cm (male)
  • Weight: proportionate to height (BMI 18–22 preferred)
  • Good communication skills in English and Hindi
  • No major visible tattoos or scars (as per airline standards)
  • Good vision (corrected vision acceptable at some airlines)
