Why Bangalore for EASA Part 66 Coaching?

Bangalore is South India's undisputed aviation training hub. The city hosts India's largest aerospace ecosystem — from ISRO and HAL to three major Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul (MRO) facilities operated by HAL, IndiGo, and Air India. This concentration of aviation industry activity makes Bangalore the natural home for specialised training such as EASA Part 66.

Critically, Bangalore is the only city in South India where you will find an EASA Part 147-partnered coaching institute. There is currently no equivalent in Coimbatore, Chennai, Madurai, Hyderabad, or Kochi. For Tamil Nadu students serious about an international AME career, travelling to Bangalore is not just an option — it is the only credible route.

Who Comes from South India for EASA Coaching?

Vajra Aviation has enrolled students from across South India — Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, and Telangana.   • Fresh B.E./B.Tech Graduates from Tamil Nadu Engineering Colleges

  • DGCA AME Licence Holders Seeking EASA Recognition
    Engineers with an existing DGCA AME licence who want to upgrade to EASA Part 66, opening access to international MRO employers in Europe, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia.

    Aviation technicians currently employed in India who cannot relocate full-time but want to study for EASA modules on weekends, progressively clearing all 13 modules over 12–24 months.
