Course Overview

The DGCA Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (AME) License is India's national certification for aircraft maintenance professionals. Issued by the Directorate General of Civil Aviation under Rule 61 of the Aircraft Rules 1937, the DGCA AME License authorises holders to certify maintenance on Indian-registered civil aircraft — a legal requirement for any maintenance certification work in India.

At Vajra Aviation, our DGCA AME Exam Training is a highly specialised program that targets engineering graduates and qualified diploma holders who need structured, exam-focused preparation to pass the rigorous DGCA AME examinations. Our Ex-IAF technical faculty and qualified AME instructors provide in-depth coverage of all examination subjects with comprehensive study material, mock tests, and individual mentoring.

Course Curriculum

All DGCA AME examination subjects covered with exam-focused depth:

Module 1: Basic Sciences & Mathematics
  • Mathematics — algebra, trigonometry, logarithms, geometry
  • Physics — mechanics, thermodynamics, electricity, magnetism
  • Electrical fundamentals — Ohm's law, AC/DC circuits, capacitors, inductors
  • Electronic fundamentals — semiconductors, transistors, logic gates
  • Digital principles as applied to aircraft systems
Module 2: Aerodynamics & Aircraft General Knowledge
  • Principles of flight — lift, drag, thrust, weight
  • Aerofoil theory — camber, chord, angle of attack
  • Aircraft stability — longitudinal, lateral, directional
  • Aircraft control surfaces and flight control systems
  • High-lift devices — flaps, slats, leading edge devices
Module 3: Airframe Structures & Systems
  • Fuselage construction — monocoque, semi-monocoque
  • Wing structures — spars, ribs, stringers, skin panels
  • Empennage and control surface construction
  • Landing gear systems — retraction, steering, braking, anti-skid
  • Doors, windows, and pressurisation structure
  • Composite structures — CFRP, GFRP, repair procedures
Module 4: Powerplant — Piston & Turbine Engines
  • Piston engine fundamentals — 4-stroke cycle, engine construction
  • Carburettors, fuel injection systems, ignition systems
  • Gas turbine engine theory — Brayton cycle, compressor, turbine
  • Turbofan, turboprop, and turboshaft engines
  • Engine starting, runup, and shutdown procedures
  • Engine health monitoring and performance trending
Module 5: Aircraft Systems — Hydraulic, Pneumatic, Fuel, Electrical
  • Hydraulic systems — components, fluids, actuators, reservoirs
  • Pneumatic systems — bleed air, APU, ground air
  • Fuel systems — tanks, pumps, transfer, jettison, fuel quantity
  • Aircraft electrical power generation — IDG, TRU, batteries
  • Pressurisation and air conditioning — pack, zone, outflow valve
  • Fire detection and protection systems
Module 6: Avionics — Instruments, Navigation & Communications
  • Pitot-static instruments — altimeter, ASI, VSI
  • Gyroscopic instruments — ADI, HSI, DI, turn coordinator
  • Air data computers and EFIS displays
  • Navigation systems — VOR, ILS, GPS, FMS, INS/IRS
  • Communication systems — VHF, HF, ACARS, SELCAL
  • Warning systems — TCAS, GPWS/TAWS, windshear detection
Module 7: Airworthiness Regulations & DGCA Rules
  • Aircraft Rules 1937 — Rule 61 (AME licensing) and related rules
  • DGCA CAR M — continuing airworthiness requirements
  • DGCA CAR 145 — approved maintenance organisation requirements
  • Airworthiness Directives (ADs) — mandatory compliance
  • Aircraft documents — CofA, CofR, maintenance records
  • Human factors in maintenance — errors, fatigue, communication
Module 8: Maintenance Practices & Practical Skills
  • Safety practices — personal safety, chemical handling, fire prevention
  • Use of hand tools, torque wrenches, and special tools
  • Reading and interpreting maintenance manuals, IPC, and wiring diagrams
  • Fasteners — bolts, nuts, rivets, quick-release fasteners
  • Corrosion identification, prevention, and treatment
  • NDT awareness — visual, DPT, magnetic particle, eddy current, ultrasonic

Eligibility Criteria

  • B.E. / B.Tech in any engineering discipline (Mechanical, Electrical, Electronics, Aeronautical preferred)
  • Diploma in Aeronautical Engineering (3-year programme from a recognised institution)
  • Ex-Indian Air Force technical personnel with aircraft maintenance experience
  • Candidates who have completed DGCA-approved AME basic training
  • Age: 18 years minimum
  • Good command of English for technical documentation
Note: The DGCA AME License requires candidates to have completed approved basic training AND acquired practical experience (typically 2–3 years) in an approved maintenance organisation before the license is issued. Our training prepares you for the DGCA examinations — practical experience must be completed separately.

Certification

Our training prepares you to pass the DGCA AME examinations. Upon clearing the required papers and completing practical experience requirements, you receive the DGCA Aircraft Maintenance Engineer License.

  • Vajra Aviation DGCA AME Exam Training Certificate
  • DGCA AME License — Category A, B, C, or E (upon examination and experience)
  • ISO 9001:2015 quality-assured training documentation

Career Outcomes

A DGCA AME License is the gateway to a technically rewarding and well-compensated career in Indian civil aviation maintenance:

  • Certifying AME at IndiGo, Air India, SpiceJet, Akasa maintenance departments
  • AME at MRO organisations — Air Works, Lufthansa Technik India, GMR Aero Technic
  • Helicopter maintenance at Pawan Hans, ONGC Aviation, HPCL Aviation
  • Career progression to Technical Manager, CAME, Quality Manager
  • Pathway to EASA Part 66 license for global opportunities (via our EASA Part 66 course)
Salary Potential

Entry-level DGCA AMEs earn ₹30,000–₹60,000/month. Experienced certifying AMEs at major airlines earn ₹80,000–₹1.5 Lakhs/month.
