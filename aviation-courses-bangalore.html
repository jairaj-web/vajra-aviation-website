FAQs

Common Questions About Aviation Courses in Bangalore

Which is the best aviation training institute in Bangalore?
Vajra Aviation Private Limited is widely recognised as Bangalore's premier aviation training institute — the only institute founded by Ex-IAF officers offering 9 aviation courses under one roof, ISO 9001:2015 certified, and India's only Aviotrace Swiss authorised partner for EASA Part 66 training.
What aviation courses are available in Bangalore?
Bangalore offers aviation courses covering: CPL Ground School, ATPL Ground School, EASA Part 66 (B1.1 and B2), DGCA AME, Cabin Crew/Air Hostess Training, Flight Dispatcher, Ground Staff Training, and Graduate Engineering (EASA). Vajra Aviation offers all 9 programmes at one centre in Amruthalli, Bangalore.
What is the eligibility for aviation courses in Bangalore?
Most aviation courses require 10+2 completion with Physics and Maths. Cabin Crew training requires minimum age 18 with 10+2. EASA Part 66 and DGCA AME accept B.Tech/Diploma graduates for fast-track entry. CPL/ATPL ground school requires students to be pursuing or holding a valid DGCA CPL.
How much do aviation courses cost in Bangalore?
Aviation course fees in Bangalore vary: CPL/ATPL ground school ₹80,000–1,50,000; EASA Part 66 training varies by modules; Cabin Crew courses ₹50,000–1,20,000; DGCA AME training ₹1,50,000–3,00,000 for the full 2-year programme. Vajra Aviation offers EMI options. Call 6364919234 for current fee structure.
Are aviation courses worth it in India?
Yes — India's aviation sector is one of the fastest-growing in the world. IndiGo, Air India, Akasa Air, and new entrants are expanding fleets rapidly, creating demand for pilots, engineers, cabin crew, dispatchers, and ground staff. EASA Part 66 engineers can additionally work across 30+ countries in Europe and the Middle East.
What is the difference between DGCA AME and EASA Part 66?
DGCA AME is India's domestic aircraft maintenance licence valid for Indian-registered aircraft. EASA Part 66 is the European standard, valid in 30+ countries including UAE, Europe, UK, and Singapore. Both require similar technical knowledge but different examinations and practical experience. Vajra Aviation offers both programmes.
How long does it take to complete an aviation course in Bangalore?
Aviation course durations at Vajra Aviation: Cabin Crew and Ground Staff — 3–6 months. CPL Ground School — 6–8 months. Flight Dispatcher — 3–6 months. ATPL Ground School — 10–12 months. DGCA AME — 24 months. EASA Part 66 B1.1/B2 — 18–24 months.
