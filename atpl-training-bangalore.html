What is the full form of ATPL?
ATPL stands for Airline Transport Pilot Licence — the highest level of pilot certification issued by DGCA India. It allows pilots to act as Pilot in Command (Captain) of commercial aircraft operated under Instrument Flight Rules. An ATPL requires clearing 13 DGCA ground school subjects, accumulating 1,500 hours of flight time, and passing skills tests.
How many subjects are there in DGCA ATPL ground school?
DGCA ATPL ground school covers 13 subjects: Air Navigation, Meteorology, Air Regulations, Technical General, Technical Specific, Human Performance & Limitations, Radio Telephony, Instruments & Communications, Flight Planning & Fuel Monitoring, Aircraft Performance, Mass & Balance, Aviation Meteorology, and DGCA Exam Preparation.
How long does ATPL ground school take at Vajra Aviation?
The ATPL ground school programme at Vajra Aviation is 10–12 months, covering all 13 DGCA subjects. The course includes module-wise classes, mock CBT tests after each subject, and a final revision phase before DGCA exams.
What is the difference between CPL and ATPL?
CPL (Commercial Pilot Licence) allows you to fly as co-pilot on commercial aircraft, while ATPL allows you to be Captain (Pilot in Command) of scheduled airline flights. ATPL requires more flight hours (1,500 vs 200 for CPL) and additional ground subjects. Many pilots start with CPL and upgrade to ATPL after gaining experience.
What is the pass mark for DGCA ATPL exams?
DGCA requires a minimum pass mark of 70% in each subject. Candidates must pass all 13 subjects to be eligible for the ATPL licence. Failed papers can be retaken after 90 days. Vajra Aviation's mock CBT tests are calibrated to the exact DGCA exam format.
Can I do ATPL ground school while working?
Yes — Vajra Aviation offers morning and weekend batches specifically designed for working professionals and pilot trainees who cannot attend weekday daytime classes. Contact us at 6364919234 to check current batch timings.
What is the salary of an airline pilot with ATPL in India?
Airline pilots (First Officers) in India earn ₹2–3 LPA at cadet level rising to ₹12–18 LPA as Captains with major carriers like IndiGo, Air India, or Akasa Air. International routes and wide-body aircraft command significantly higher packages. ATPL holders are eligible for Captain upgrades typically after 3,000–5,000 hours of flying experience.
