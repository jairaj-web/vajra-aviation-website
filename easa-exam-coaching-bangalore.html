What is EASA Part 66 Exam Coaching?

EASA Part 66 exam coaching is structured preparation for the EASA Part 66 aircraft maintenance engineer (AME) module examinations. The EASA Part 66 licence requires candidates to pass up to 22 written modules — each covering a specific technical subject such as Mathematics, Physics, Aerodynamics, Aircraft Structures, Electrical Fundamentals, or Avionics. Exams are conducted in multiple choice question (MCQ) format with a 75% pass mark. In India, coaching is provided by EASA Part 147-approved training organisations or their official partners. Vajra Aviation in Bangalore, through its partnership with Aviotrace Swiss — an EASA Part 147 MTO based in Switzerland — provides India's most credible EASA exam coaching programme, with all 22 modules successfully cleared by students.

Unlike generic coaching centres, EASA Part 66 exam preparation demands deep subject expertise aligned to EASA's own syllabus. Every module has a defined Level 1, Level 2, or Level 3 depth of knowledge requirement. Vajra Aviation's faculty — drawn from the Indian Air Force and civil aviation — deliver coaching calibrated precisely to EASA's published learning objectives, helping students avoid the most common causes of failure: insufficient MCQ practice, poor time management, and inadequate coverage of fringe topics.

Vajra Aviation's coaching program is built on one principle: first-attempt success. Our structured module schedule, performance-tracked mock tests, and focused revision sessions have produced consistently strong results since the institute was founded in 2013 by Ex-IAF officers.

Our 4-Step Exam Preparation Approach

Every student joining Vajra Aviation's EASA exam coaching programme goes through a structured four-step journey designed to maximise first-attempt pass rates. Our approach is shaped by years of experience converting Ex-IAF technicians and engineering graduates into EASA-licensed professionals.

STEP 1

Module Assessment

We begin with a detailed assessment of your academic and professional background — whether you are a fresh engineering graduate, a DGCA-licensed AME seeking global recognition, or an Ex-IAF technician. Based on this, we identify exactly which EASA modules you need to clear, map out an optimal study sequence, and advise on any potential exemptions available through your prior qualifications.

STEP 2

Structured Classroom Coaching

Module-by-module classes are conducted by our Ex-IAF faculty, covering the complete EASA syllabus at the correct knowledge level (Level 1, 2, or 3) for each topic. Classes are held on weekdays and weekends to suit working professionals. Comprehensive study notes aligned to EASA's published syllabi are provided for every module, eliminating reliance on generic textbooks.

STEP 3

MCQ Practice & Mock Tests

After each module's teaching phase, students undergo intensive MCQ practice using a curated question bank built around real EASA exam patterns. Weekly timed mock tests replicate actual exam conditions — with per-question time limits, performance scoring, and detailed review of wrong answers. Students who score below 80% in mocks receive additional targeted coaching before sitting the real module exam.

STEP 4

Exam Strategy & Revision

In the final phase before each module exam, instructors guide students through exam strategy: how to eliminate wrong options, manage time effectively, handle unfamiliar question types, and avoid the common traps that cause candidates to drop below the 75% threshold. Final revision sessions consolidate high-weightage topics and common areas of confusion identified from mock test data.
