How to Become a Commercial Pilot in India — Step by Step

Becoming a commercial pilot in India requires completing several steps in a defined order. Here is the complete pathway from 10+2 to your first airline job:

1
Complete 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics
You must have passed 10+2 (HSC) with Physics and Maths as core subjects from any recognised board. Students appearing in final exams may apply provisionally.
2
Obtain a DGCA Class 1 Medical Certificate
A Class 1 Medical Certificate from a DGCA-approved Aviation Medical Examiner is mandatory for CPL issue. Check vision, hearing, and general health early — it saves time later.
3
Complete CPL Ground School — All 7 DGCA Subjects
Join a DGCA-aligned CPL ground school in Bangalore like Vajra Aviation to study all 7 subjects: Air Navigation, Meteorology, Air Regulations, Technical General, Technical Specific, Flight Planning, and Radio Telephony. Takes 4–6 months. Can be done before or alongside flight training.
4
Clear All 7 DGCA CPL Written Examinations
DGCA conducts CPL written exams at designated test centres. Each subject requires 70% minimum. Exams are MCQ-based — 50 questions per paper. You have up to 6 attempts per subject. Vajra Aviation's structured mock tests deliver 95%+ first-attempt success.
5
Complete 200 Flying Hours at a DGCA-Approved FTO
Complete a minimum of 200 flight hours at a DGCA-approved Flying Training Organisation — including 100 hours pilot-in-command, cross-country, and instrument flying. Flight training costs ₹35–60 lakhs depending on the school and country.
6
Pass the DGCA CPL Skill Test
A DGCA examiner evaluates your takeoff, landing, navigation, instrument procedures, and emergency handling in an actual aircraft. Passing qualifies you for CPL issue.
7
Receive Your DGCA CPL and Apply to Airlines
With CPL in hand you are eligible for First Officer roles at IndiGo, Air India, Akasa Air, SpiceJet, and more. Most airlines require 200+ hours and ATPL theory for multi-crew aircraft operations.
Expert Tip from Vajra Aviation Faculty

Complete ground school first. Clearing your 7 DGCA papers before starting flight training saves 6–12 months. Students who delay ground school often sit idle after completing their flight hours, waiting for exams.

Course Curriculum

All 7 DGCA CPL subjects covered in a structured, exam-focused sequence:

Module 1: Air Navigation
  • Radio Navigation systems — VOR, NDB, ILS, DME
  • GPS / GNSS principles and practical applications
  • Chart reading, plotting, and dead reckoning
  • Time and distance calculations
  • DGCA exam question analysis and practice
Module 2: Meteorology
  • Atmospheric structure, temperature and pressure
  • Weather systems — fronts, cyclones, anti-cyclones
  • Turbulence, icing, and severe weather phenomena
  • METAR, TAF, and SIGMET reading and interpretation
  • Aviation weather reports and forecasts
Module 3: Air Regulations
  • ICAO Annexes 1–18 — scope and applicability
  • DGCA CAR (Civil Aviation Requirements) series
  • Aircraft registration, airworthiness, and licensing
  • Air traffic rules and procedures
  • Rules of the Air — SERA and Indian rules
Module 4: Technical General
  • Airframe structures — fuselage, wings, empennage
  • Aircraft systems — hydraulics, pneumatics, electrical
  • Powerplant fundamentals — piston and turbine engines
  • Fuel systems and oil systems
  • Flight instruments — pitot-static and gyroscopic
Module 5: Technical Specific
  • Aircraft type-specific systems (as applicable to endorsed type)
  • Autopilot and flight management systems
  • Avionics — communication and navigation equipment
  • Emergency systems and procedures
  • Performance data and limitations
Module 6: Flight Planning & Fuel
  • Fuel calculation methods and trip fuel planning
  • Mass and Balance — centre of gravity calculations
  • Alternate aerodrome selection criteria
  • IFR flight plan filing and ATC coordination
  • NOTAM interpretation and pre-flight planning
Module 7: Radio Telephony (RTR)
  • ICAO standard phraseology and procedures
  • Aerodrome, approach, and en-route communications
  • Emergency and urgency radio procedures
  • Communication failure procedures
  • RTR examination preparation and mock tests

DGCA CPL Exam Guide — All 7 Subjects Explained

Each DGCA CPL paper has a specific focus, question count, and pass threshold. Here is what each subject tests and how Vajra Aviation prepares you:

Subject Questions Pass Mark Key Topics Difficulty
Air Navigation 50 MCQs 70% Radio navaids (VOR, NDB, ILS, DME), chart reading, dead reckoning, GPS, RNAV High
Meteorology 50 MCQs 70% Atmosphere, weather systems, METAR/TAF, icing, turbulence, fronts Medium
Air Regulations 50 MCQs 70% ICAO Annexes 1–18, DGCA CARs, aircraft registration, ATC rules, rules of the air Medium
Technical General 50 MCQs 70% Airframe structures, hydraulics, electrics, engines, instruments, fuel systems High
Technical Specific 50 MCQs 70% Type-specific systems, FMS, autopilot, avionics, emergency systems, performance data Medium
Flight Planning & Fuel 50 MCQs 70% Fuel calculations, mass & balance, IFR planning, alternates, NOTAM interpretation High
Radio Telephony (RTR) Oral + Written 70% ICAO phraseology, ATC procedures, emergency communications, R/T practice Lower

All 7 subjects must be cleared within 3 years from clearing the first subject. Vajra Aviation's question bank covers 2,000+ practice questions across all 7 papers.

Common Questions

CPL Ground School FAQs

What is CPL ground school?

CPL (Commercial Pilot Licence) ground school is the theory training required to clear DGCA CPL written examinations. It covers 7 subjects: Air Navigation, Meteorology, Air Regulations, Technical General, Technical Specific, Flight Planning & Fuel Management, and Radio Telephony. Ground school is separate from flight training and can be completed in Bangalore at Vajra Aviation.

What are the eligibility criteria for CPL in India?

To pursue a CPL in India you must be at least 17 years old (18 for licence issue), have passed 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics, hold a Class 1 DGCA Medical Certificate, and be an Indian citizen. Ground school can be started before completing flight hours.

How long does CPL ground school take?

CPL ground school at Vajra Aviation takes 4–6 months. The regular batch (Mon–Sat, 9AM–1PM) takes approximately 6 months. The fast-track batch (Mon–Sat, 9AM–5PM) takes approximately 4 months and is ideal for career changers or students who want to complete quickly.

How many subjects are there in the DGCA CPL exam?

The DGCA CPL examination covers 7 subjects: Air Navigation, Meteorology, Air Regulations, Technical General, Technical Specific, Flight Planning & Fuel Management (FPFM), and Radio Telephony (RTR). All subjects must be cleared before a CPL can be issued.

Can I do CPL ground school without joining a flying school?

Yes. CPL ground school (theory training) is entirely separate from flight training. You can complete ground school at Vajra Aviation in Bangalore and do your flying hours at any DGCA-approved flying school in India. This is the most cost-effective approach for most students.
